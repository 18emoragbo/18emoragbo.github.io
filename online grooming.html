Online Grooming

What Is Online Grooming?

Online grooming is when someone uses the internet to trick, force or pressure a young person into doing something sexual - like sending a naked video or image of themselves. Someone who’s grooming others online will sometimes build their trust before talking about doing anything sexual.

The Stages Of Grooming (In the perspective of the attacker)

  • - The Friendship Forming Stage: This is when the attacker finds a victim, and starts to gain their trust
  • - The Relationship Forming Stage: This is when the attacker starts to find out from the child about the things that they/ their family need, and the attacker then fills that need
  • - The Risk Assessment Stage: This is when the attacker asks the victim questions, to find out how closely monitored they are online, and to find out how close the child's relationship is with their family
  • - The Exclusivity Stage: This is when the attacker tries to gain complete trust of the child, and makes the child believe that they share some sort of special bond, and they introduce the concepts of love and care
  • - The Sexual Stage: This is when the predator starts to ask questions about the victim's sexuality, and introduce them to certain language/ content
  • - The Conclusion Stage: This is when the attacker starts to physically abuse the child, and they start to use threats to control the child, and to keep the child from saying anything
